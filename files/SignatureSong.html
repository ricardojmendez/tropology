Signature Song

A music trope for the (usually) one song by an artist that everyone knows. It doesn't matter how people know the song — maybe the song was used in a movie, maybe it was used in a TV show, maybe it was used for an ad that got played over and over and over again — but people know it.

These songs do tend to be Ear Worms, songs with an Epic Riff and can also be Black Sheep Hits. It's also possible for an artist to have more than one Signature Song, especially one for two or more distinct phases of their career. Whatever the case, these songs are very likely to be awesome.

Some of the more rabid members of the artist's fandom community may accuse one of not being a true fan for disliking the artist's Signature Song, even if said person may like every other song that the artist has recorded — or, inversely, liking the Signature Song more than what Fanon considers the artist's "real best work."

Sometimes the signature song isn't the artist's best-charting hit. It also doesn't necessarily run in line with their Signature Style.

The song can also be famous because of a certain work. The songs that are Potholed are examples of these.

If they made an appearance in Guitar Hero or Rock Band, it's probably this song. If the artist is a composer of Classical Music, the song has probably become a Standard Snippet.

In earlier times, such as the 1930s and 1940s, it was not uncommon for a band or singer to open their set with their signature song or at least a few bars of it, especially when they appeared on radio. With the advent of television talk shows it also became not uncommon for the studio band to play a few bars from the artist's signature song as he or she walked onstage.

To stop people from leaving at gigs after they've heard the song note as what happened a lot to Radiohead with their song "Creep" from Pablo Honey before they developed a larger fandom, these songs are normally played live either at the end of the main set or during the encore. Not performing it at all may result in an Iconic Song Request.

Some musicians arguably avert this by having several equally famous hit songs, The Beatles, Michael Jackson and Elvis Presley being three prominent examples. If they'd left off one of their hits from a show it wouldn't have been such an obvious oversight. That said, certainly some of their songs are more identified with them than others.

Occasionally, an artist could have a signature song that is eventually usurped by another song. It's also possible for an artist to have different signature songs in different countries.

Often overlaps with Black Sheep Hit. See also One-Hit Wonder, for an extreme case of this trope. Not to be confused with Leitmotif or Character Signature Song, which is about signature songs sang by fictional characters.

Artists are listed alphabetically. Signature songs for films and musicals are listed after all the artists.
